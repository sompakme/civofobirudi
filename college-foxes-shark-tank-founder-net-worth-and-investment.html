What are College Foxes Packing Boxes?

College Foxes Packing Boxes was a promotional concept created by Omar Soliman and Nick Friedman to enhance their main moving business, College Hunks Hauling Junks and Moving. It employed college-aged women, known as “foxes,” to provide packing and moving services. While a deal on Shark Tank would have been exciting, the main objective of College Foxes was to promote their established business rather than solely focus on the new venture. The founders aimed to leverage the platform of Shark Tank to elevate their primary enterprise.

“The main purpose of College Foxes Packing Boxes was to promote our existing business, College Hunks Hauling Junks and Moving. We saw an opportunity to create an additional service that could complement our primary offering and attract new customers,” said Omar Soliman, Co-founder of College Foxes Packing Boxes.

College Foxes Packing Boxes played a pivotal role in college entrepreneurship and business promotion. By utilizing college-aged women as packers and movers, the founders tapped into a unique market segment while also empowering young individuals to gain practical business experience. The strategic decision to showcase College Foxes on Shark Tank aimed to create brand awareness and generate interest in the overall College Hunks Hauling Junks and Moving brand.

Founder of College Foxes Packing Boxes

Omar Soliman and Nick Friedman are the founders of College Foxes Packing Boxes. They are also the CEO and President of College Hunks Hauling Junk. As the masterminds behind these successful ventures, Soliman and Friedman have demonstrated innovative thinking, strategic approaches, and a passion for the moving and logistics industry.

Both Soliman and Friedman originated the moving business as an extension of their primary company, College Hunks Hauling Junk. College Foxes Packing Boxes Before Shark Tank

Before their appearance on Shark Tank, College Foxes Packing Boxes did not exist as a separate entity. Omar Soliman and Nick Friedman, the founders of College Foxes, already had a successful business called College Hunks Hauling Junk. It was this thriving enterprise that inspired them to introduce the spin-off concept of College Foxes to the show.

The journey of College Hunks Hauling Junk began when Omar started hauling furniture during his college break. Recognizing the potential of this business idea, he shared it with his friend Nick, and they quickly jumped on board. Their entrepreneurial spirit and hard work paid off, as they eventually won an entrepreneurship competition and experienced rapid growth.

“The idea is simple—taking the stress out of moving and making the whole process fun,” said Omar Soliman, co-founder of College Hunks Hauling Junk.

Attracting franchisees and achieving millionaire status, College Hunks Hauling Junk became a well-established name in the moving and logistics industry. Building on this success, the founders decided to create College Foxes Packing Boxes as a promotional concept to further elevate their primary venture.

By leveraging the widespread reach and exposure provided by Shark Tank, the founders aimed to showcase not only their innovative moving company but also the concept of College Foxes Packing Boxes. Their vision was to utilize the new venture as a means to enhance their established business and attract more customers.

Throughout their business history, Omar Soliman and Nick Friedman have demonstrated their business acumen and strategic thinking in the moving and logistics industry. While College Foxes may not have become a separate entity following their appearance on Shark Tank, it remains a testament to the founders’ drive for innovation and their commitment to the success of College Hunks Hauling Junk.

College Foxes Packing Boxes on Shark Tank: Pitch, Funding Asked, Funding Received

During their appearance on Shark Tank, Omar Soliman and Nick Friedman pitched College Foxes Packing Boxes, seeking a $250,000 investment for a 25% stake. The sharks were more interested in their established business, College Hunks Hauling Junk, than the new venture. Kevin O’Leary offered a deal with a majority stake in both companies, but the founders declined. Robert Herjavec was impressed by their defense of their company and offered a deal for 50% ownership of College Foxes and 10% ownership of College Hunks as collateral. However, the founders turned down the offer and left without a deal.

Despite their passion for College Foxes Packing Boxes, Omar and Nick ultimately decided to focus on the growth and expansion of their primary business, College Hunks Hauling Junk. They recognized the importance of capitalizing on the success and potential of their established brand rather than spreading their resources too thin.

The intriguing Shark Tank pitch garnered attention and highlighted the entrepreneurial spirit of the founders. While College Foxes Packing Boxes did not secure funding on the show, their appearance served as valuable exposure and a testament to their business acumen.

“We believed in College Foxes, but ultimately, the sharks saw the greater potential in College Hunks Hauling Junk,” said Omar Soliman, co-founder of College Foxes Packing Boxes.

Although the founders left without a deal, their appearance on Shark Tank undoubtedly left a lasting impression on viewers and showcased their impressive vision and dedication to their businesses. The experience propelled Omar and Nick further in their entrepreneurial journey and opened doors for future opportunities.

What Happened to College Foxes Packing Boxes After Shark Tank?

After their appearance on Shark Tank, the future of College Foxes Packing Boxes took an unexpected turn. The founders, Omar Soliman and Nick Friedman, made the strategic decision to shift their focus towards building their primary business, College Hunks Hauling Junk. As a result, the concept of College Foxes became secondary, with plans for its further development left behind.

While College Foxes did not flourish as anticipated, College Hunks Hauling Junk continued to experience remarkable growth. Currently, the business operates from a staggering 150 franchise locations across the United States and Canada, showcasing its widespread success in the junk removal and moving industry. With an estimated annual revenue of approximately $200 million, College Hunks Hauling Junk’s achievements stand as a testament to the founders’ dedication and expertise.

The founders’ decision to prioritize College Hunks Hauling Junk over College Foxes Packing Boxes reflects their astute business acumen and their ability to adapt to market trends. By focusing their efforts on expanding their primary business, Soliman and Friedman were able to drive College Hunks to new heights, solidifying its position as a leader in the industry.

How do College Foxes Packing Boxes Make Money?

College Foxes Packing Boxes, similar to its parent company College Hunks Hauling Junk, generated revenue through various income sources. These included franchise fees, removal charges, relocation costs, and providing assistance with residential moves. By offering packing and moving services, College Foxes charged hourly rates, allowing customers to receive professional help in their relocation endeavors.

This revenue model enabled College Foxes Packing Boxes to establish a sustainable income stream while leveraging their connection to the moving industry. Additionally, the possibility of incorporating junk hauling services into their business further expanded their potential earning opportunities.
