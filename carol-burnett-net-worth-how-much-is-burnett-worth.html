The Carol Burnett Show

“The Carol Burnett Show” is the most iconic achievement of Carol Burnett’s career. The variety sketch program aired from 1967 to 1978 and was a trailblazer in many ways. It was one of the first shows to be hosted by a female host and had a significant impact on future generations of women in comedy. The show featured memorable comedy sketches, musical numbers, and interaction with the audience. It garnered widespread acclaim and won 23 Emmy Awards during its run.

One of the highlights of “The Carol Burnett Show” was the incredible comedic chemistry between Carol Burnett and her fellow cast members, including Harvey Korman, Vicki Lawrence, and Tim Conway. Their hilarious sketches, such as the famous “Gone with the Wind” parody, “Went with the Wind,” became iconic moments in television history. The show also featured guest appearances by some of the biggest names in entertainment, including Lucille Ball, Sammy Davis Jr., and Bing Crosby.

“I’m so grateful for the opportunity to have been a part of ‘The Carol Burnett Show.’ We had so much fun creating comedy and bringing laughter to our audience. It was truly a one-of-a-kind experience,” Carol Burnett reminisces.
