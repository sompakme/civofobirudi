50 Cent Says His Son, Sire Jackson, WIll Pursue A Modeling Career

 Posted on September 1, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  245 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Sire Jackson is barely out of potty trainers, but already he has a successful modeling career. 50 Cent recently stopped by “The Angie Martinez Show” and gushed over his two year-old son with model Daphne Joy. He tells the radio host that the toddler is already getting checks as a model and has landed his first lucrative deal with the first payment of $700,000. The contact is with Kidz Safe, the kids’ headphones brand under SMS audio, owned by 50 Cent. Sire will be the face of the outlet.

He said:

“I have so much fun with him, man; even my spirit change. I light up when I talk about him. He’s at that point where he repeats everything,” he said. “He’s got a [modeling] deal now with Kidz Safe. It’s an audio company. They paid him a big payment too, $700,000. He’s super cute! You gotta pay for it.”

On the topic of where the dividends will land, the Queens native said, “[The money] goes into a trust. He can’t even touch it until he turns 18. He’ll get $700,000 to be the face of the company. Already, he’s making his own money. He makes his own money. I was ready to say, ‘This is your college fund.’ But I gotta handle that part and let him have the [$700,000].”

Looks like we’ll be seeing more of baby Sire and all of his adorableness.
