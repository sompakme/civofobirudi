Georgia State Employee Salary Database

The Georgia Department of Labor maintains a comprehensive salary database for state employees. This database provides valuable information on the average number of establishments, employment figures, and weekly wages for different positions within the state. The salary data is compiled from reports submitted by employers who adhere to Georgia’s Employment Security Law, ensuring the accuracy and reliability of the information.

The confidentiality of individual employers is strictly protected, and the data is not disclosed for industries with fewer than three establishments or where one establishment accounts for 80% or more of the employment within the group.

Having access to the Georgia State Employee Salary Database is essential for state employees, policymakers, and researchers who seek to understand compensation trends and make informed decisions. This comprehensive resource plays a crucial role in promoting transparency and supporting fair and equitable compensation practices.

Average Salaries of Georgia State Employees

The average salaries of Georgia state employees vary across different industries and positions. According to the Georgia Employment and Wages report, the goods producing sector has an average weekly wage of $1,309, while the service producing sector has an average weekly wage of $1,276. Other industries with high average weekly wages include information ($2,458), finance & insurance ($2,152), and management: companies/enterprises ($2,773). These figures provide a glimpse into the compensation levels for state employees in Georgia.
