16 and Pregnant Premiere Video: Maddy Has a Baby After One-Night Stand

 Posted on August 14, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  484 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Not exactly a teenage dream. Maddy Godsey‘s one-night stand turned into a lifelong commitment when she got pregnant with Cody Jensen‘s baby after sleeping with him the day they met. Now, as they navigate first-time parenthood, they’re realizing how little they actually know about each other. In this exclusive clip from the 16 and Pregnant season five premiere, Maddy, 17, and her baby daddy, 19, go on their first official date together — and end up arguing over how to raise their daughter.

Unfortunately for baby Aubrey Lynn — born on Oct. 20 of last year — things have only gotten worse since then. Us Weekly caught up with Maddy before MTV’s Monday, April 14, premiere to get the scoop on her current relationship (or lack thereof) with Aubrey’s dad, who she says hasn’t seen his little girl in almost four months.

Asked about her pregnancy with Aubrey, Maddy told Us, “I think I was more terrified than anything else. Just because I knew my life was gonna change, I didn’t know how to tell my parents, and I knew I couldn’t support a baby by myself. It was worse because me and Cody didn’t know each other at all,” she added. “The first day we met was when I got pregnant.”

Maddy claimed she was “basically on [her] own” the whole time. “He’d be out partying while I was at home crying my eyes out because I was so lonely and didn’t know what to do,” she told Us. “And then I finally talked to him about it, and I had a breakdown and he saw me cry in person, and then he started stepping up a little bit more and helping out…But then once [Aubrey] was born, he just stopped coming around.”

“He hasn’t seen her since she was 2 months old, or asked about her,” Maddy said. She claimed, too, that she’d reached out to his family to find out about allergies on their side, but has yet to hear back. (They did, however, transform their dining room into a bedroom and offer to take in Aubrey; Maddy turned them down.)

Luckily, she has a good support system in her own parents. Her dad, she told Us, is like a father to Aubrey, and she talks to her mom “like 13 times” daily. “We talk all day, constantly, so we’re still best friends,” Maddy said.
