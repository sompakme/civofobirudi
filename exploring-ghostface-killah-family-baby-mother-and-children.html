Ghostface Killah family includes his wife, children, and his hip-hop group, Wu-Tang Clan. Killah rose to fame after the success of the rap group in the late 1990s.

The artist’s name was adopted from one of his favorite Kung Fu movies, “The Mystery of Chess Boxing.” The rapper is also the founder of Starks Enterprises. Killah is known for his loud, fast-paced, and emotional rhetoric of rapping style.

Killa is quite popular in terms of his musical performance and talent. He is a New York rapper like Melvoni, Lil Tecca, and Pasto Flocco.

Ghostface was also a founding member of the Wu-Tang Clan. He and his roommate RZA founded the group with seven members in 1995. The crew released their first album Wu-Tang (36 Chambers). Similarly, the rapper’s first solo album was Ironman (1996).

The rappers of the Clan were able to get fame through their career as a group than solo performances. However, their leader RZA made a large career as a record producer, filmmaker, and artist through the clan. 

Similarly, Ghostface Killah wife and children were other empowering figures for him to pursue his artistic endeavor in his career. 

Ghostface Killah Children

Ghostface Killah has four children. His sons names are Dennis Ames, Infinite Coles, and Supreme Intelligence. Killah’s two oldest sons are rappers, while his youngest chose the route of R&B.

Killah has a good relationship with his oldest, Dennis Ames, aka Son God. The two of them collaborated on a song titled “The Chase,” “Man Up,” “Street Opera,” and many others. The rapper also has a daughter; she seems to be a very private person. 

Sun God 

Sun God is a rising rapper from New York; he is a 29-year-old up-and-coming rapper who has released multiple singles, including songs like Vigilante, 2nd Generation Wu, and multiple collaborations with his father. 

The artist banded together with a group called 2nd generation Wu-Tang. The members include GFTD- Intell (Son of U-God), PXWER (Method man’s son), and Young Dirty Bastard (Son of the late Old Dirty Bastard), along with his brother Supreme Intelligence. The group split off from the Wu-Tang family and founded their branch.

Dennis Ames’s popular songs include Gunshowers, Lifestyles, Colorful Noise, Slick Shit, Bomb Threat, and Die 4 My Team. The artist has released multiple tracks in collaboration with 2nd generation Wu in their discography.

Supreme Intelligence

Supreme Intelligence is the second son of Ghostface Killah. He might not have gained notoriety for his rapping career, but the artist has released multiple songs and albums.

The famous song of Supreme intelligence includes Guess Who, Mental Stimulus, Touch the Sky, Prince Akeem, My Gang, The Dead Don’t Cry, Cold Summer, and BlackHoodie Music.

The artist seems to have the technical skill, flow, and ability to achieve mainstream attention, but he lacks marketing, as the artist is one of the underrated rappers from New York.

The rapper also went to Morgan State University in Baltimore, Maryland. He majored in Sociology which helped him understand different cultural norms in life. 

Infinite Coles 

The youngest son of Killah has taken a different route than his son. Coles’s music includes R&B and soul singing style rather than his father’s legacy of rapping.

The rapper’s EPs include Destiny and Infidel. The singer’s popular songs include Infidel, Rapture, Round, No Room, Lightning, Spiders, and Destiny. The artist is yet to get mainstream popularity, but he is grabbing the audience’s attention through his unique songs and performances.
