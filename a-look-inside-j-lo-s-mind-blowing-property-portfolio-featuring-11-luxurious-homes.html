Jennifer Lopez knew she wanted to become an artist. From a young age, she indulged in the world of dance, acting, and singing. After getting her big break in Hollywood through a dance TV show, Lopez expanded her reach by becoming a seasoned singer who has sold millions of copies worldwide. Lopez has garnered a $400 million net worth through hard work and dedication. While the actress likes to spend her money on fashion and vacationing with her children, she also has a stunning real estate portfolio that dates back to the first mansion she purchased in LA after her stint in TV shows and becoming Janet Jackson’s backup dancer.

From a Hidden Hills home to a luxurious condo in New York, Jennifer Lopez has a good eye for purchasing stunning homes that are contemporary yet cozy. Let’s look at her real estate portfolio that features 11 properties.

The Summit Mansion, LA

In a gated community called Summit, Jennifer Lopez bought her first property in LA in 2000. She paid $4.3 million for the seven-bedroom and seven-and-a-half-bathroom home. The main house featured a sleek and modern interior that reminds one of a museum. The home also had an infinity-edge swimming pool, playground, tennis court, and a chicken coop, along with a guest house. Lopez sold the home in 2004 for $10.95 million.

First Miami Home

A Spanish-style beachfront villa, Jennifer Lopez purchased her first home in Miami in 2002 for an undisclosed amount. The 1.2-acre of land has six bedrooms and eight-and-a-half bathrooms with a koi pond, bay frontage, and a 60-foot swimming pool. The home's last owner was singer Phil Collins who sold the house for $40 million, as noted by Architectural Digest.

Brookville Mansion, Long Island

After Lopez married singer Marc Anthony in 2004, she moved into his Long Island Georgian-style brick mansion and bought the home across the street for $2 million. The couple combined the houses to create a lovely villa where they raised their twins Emme and Max. The couple put the property on sale in 2014 after their split and sold it for $4.5 million in 2017.

Hidden Hills Estate, LA

Jennifer Lopez purchased a massive home in 2017 in the Hidden Hills area in 2010 for $8.2 million. The house was built in a traditional style and divided into two structures. It has nine bedrooms and nine bathrooms. The additional rooms in the home include a recording studio, a dance studio, a full-service bar, and a 20-seat theater. Lopez initially put the house on sale for $17.5 million in 2015; however, it sold in 2017 for $10 million.

Hampton Home

A home that is still in her real estate portfolio, the singer purchased her secluded Hampton Home for $10 million in 2013. The home has an 8,660 sq. ft. of area and contains eight bedrooms, a kids’ play area, a pool, a home theatre, a steam room, and a sauna.

Manhattan Penthouse

Jennifer Lopez purchased a $20 million penthouse in 2014 in Manhattan. The family-sized home with a Madison Square Park view has four bedrooms and six bathrooms. It had wooden furnishings and marble bathrooms with a chic interior. She put it on sale for $26.5 million in 2017 but found no buyer, as stated by NY Post.

Bel Air Compound

Purchased from actress Sela Ward for $28 million, Lopez bought the French country-style estate in 2016. Initially built in 1940 by architect Samuel Marx, the home has been renovated and features a koi pond, man-made swimming pool, and 100-seat amphitheater.

Park Avenue Condo

When Jennifer Lopez began dating Alex Rodriguez, the couple took a big step and purchased a Park Avenue Condo for $15.3 million. The high-floor unit only had three bedrooms; however, the couple wanted more space for the family. They sold it for $17.5 million in 2019.

Malibu Beach House

After selling their Park Avenue condo, Lopez and Rodriguez bought a three-story Malibu beach house for $6.6 million. It offered the view of the Pacific Ocean from the four bedrooms. They sold the home for $6.775 million in September 2020.

Encino Home, LA

The third property the couple bought together was a modest three-bedroom home in Encino for $1.4 million, as stated by Observer. The house is one of her smallest properties and was purchased as an investment opportunity during the pandemic in 2020.

Star Island Home

The priciest investment Lopez made was a waterfront Star Island home she purchased with Alex Rodriguez for $32.5 million. It is shrouded in Palm trees, has direct access to Biscayne Bay, and offers a magnificent view of the Miami skyline. The home has ten bedrooms and 12 bathrooms, a light-filled kitchen, and a private elevator. Other amenities include an expansive infinity pool and a wine room. While the couple has broken up, they still have joint custody of the home.

From living in a small home in the Bronx to having owned over 11 properties, Jennifer Lopez has one of Hollywood's most luxurious real estate portfolios. Her mind-blowing mega-mansions are equipped with every luxury amenity possible for the star to relax after working on sets. She will next be seen starring in a 2022 romance movie, Marry Me.
